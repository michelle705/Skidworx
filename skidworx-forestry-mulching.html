The Process

How Forestry Mulching Works

Unlike traditional land clearing that requires multiple machines and days of hauling, forestry mulching is a single-step process that leaves your land clean and immediately usable.

01

Site Assessment & Free Estimate

We visit your property, assess the vegetation density, tree sizes, terrain, and access points. You get a clear, honest quote with no hidden fees — typically within 24 hours of your call.

02

Equipment Mobilization

Our CAT skid steer equipped with a high-flow forestry mulching head is transported to your property. The machine is purpose-built to handle Florida's dense, fibrous vegetation including palmetto and Brazilian Pepper.

03

Single-Pass Clearing

The rotating carbide-tooth drum cuts through trees up to 10–12 inches in diameter, shrubs, stumps, and root systems — grinding everything into a fine mulch layer deposited directly on the ground in a single pass.

04

Ready-to-Use Land

When we leave, your land is clear, topsoil-intact, and immediately ready for fencing, construction, farming, or recreational use. The mulch layer naturally decomposes and suppresses future weed growth — no cleanup required.

Common Questions

Forestry Mulching FAQ

What does forestry mulching cost per acre in Florida?
Forestry mulching in Florida typically runs $150–$400 per acre depending on vegetation density, tree size, terrain, and site accessibility. SkidWorx provides free on-site estimates with a firm quote — no surprises. Call (352) 206-6203.
How is forestry mulching different from bush hogging?
Bush hogging cuts and lays vegetation flat on the surface. Forestry mulching uses a high-powered rotating drum with carbide teeth to completely grind everything — including stumps and surface roots — into fine mulch. The result is a clean, level surface vs. a field of cut stems and debris.
What types of vegetation can forestry mulching handle?
Our CAT equipment handles palmetto scrub, Brazilian Pepper, sand live oak, pine, gallberry, wax myrtle, cabbage palm, willow, privet, and most Florida understory vegetation. We can process trees up to 10–12 inches in diameter in a single pass.
Do I need any permits for forestry mulching in Pasco County?
Generally no permits are required for forestry mulching on private property in Pasco County — and unlike burning, there are no fire permits needed. If your parcel is adjacent to a wetland or conservation area, additional rules may apply. We'll advise during your free estimate.
Will forestry mulching kill Brazilian Pepper permanently?
Forestry mulching removes all above-ground growth effectively. Brazilian Pepper can re-sprout from root systems over time. For permanent eradication, combining mulching with a follow-up herbicide application on new growth is the most effective long-term approach.
